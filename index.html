About Us

Together for Good is dedicated to raising awareness and providing support for people who face social discrimination and issues. Our mission is to create a safe and inclusive space where people can share their experiences, learn from others, and get help from our volunteers. Whether you are facing discrimination based on your race, gender, sexuality, disability, religion, or any other factor, we are here to listen and help. You can use our site to:

  • Tell us about the social discrimination or issues you are facing and request help from our volunteers. We will try our best to connect you with the right resources and services that can assist you.
  • Read inspiring stories of people who overcame social discrimination and succeeded in various fields. You can also share your own story and let others know how you dealt with the challenges and achieved your goals.
  • Join our community of people who care about social equality and want to make a difference.

We believe that everyone deserves respect, dignity, and equal opportunities in life. We hope that our site can help you overcome the barriers that hold you back and empower you to pursue your dreams. Join us today and be part of the change!

Jackie Robinson


He was the first African American to play in Major League Baseball in the modern era. He faced racism and hostility from fans, opponents, and even some teammates.


When Jackie Robinson took his position at first base at Ebbets Field on April 15, 1947, American history changed forever. Becoming the first African-American in Major League Baseball in the 20th century, Robinson faced harsh criticism from fans, other players, and even his own teammates. As the season waned, however, the criticism diminished, and the praise grew.

Nick Vujicic


Born without arms or legs, Nick faced enormous challenges from a very young age. Growing up, he experienced intense bullying and discrimination, which left him feeling isolated and hopeless.


Despite his obstacles, Nick refused to give up on his dreams. With the help of his supportive family and a deep faith in God, he worked tirelessly to overcome his physical limitations and achieve his goals. He learned to swim, surf, and even play golf using only his feet.

Today, Nick is a highly sought-after motivational speaker, spreading his message of hope and resilience to audiences all over the world. He has authored several books, including the best-selling Life Without Limits, and has been featured in numerous documentaries and TV shows.

Maya Angelou


Maya Angelou was an American memoirist, poet, and civil rights activist.As an African American, Angelou experienced firsthand racial prejudices and discrimination in Arkansas. She also suffered at the hands of a family associate around the age of 7


A renowned American memoirist, poet, and civil rights activist, overcame significant struggles to become an inspirational figure. At the age of seven, she endured a traumatic incident that left her mute for five years. Growing up in a racially segregated society, Angelou faced discrimination and witnessed racial violence. However, her love for literature and poetry helped her find healing and resilience. Angelou's groundbreaking memoir, "I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings," brought her international recognition, shedding light on her journey of self-discovery and the enduring impact of racism. Throughout her life, she remained an influential voice for civil rights, using her powerful words to advocate for social justice. Maya Angelou's legacy serves as a reminder of the strength of the human spirit and the transformative power of art and activism.
